Why I’m Writing This

My love,

There’s something I need to put into words, not because something is broken between us, but because I don’t want anything unspoken sitting quietly in the corners of our marriage.

These past two months have been beautiful, and they have also brought up things in me I didn’t expect. Becoming your husband has forced me to see my childhood more clearly. I grew up emotionally filling a space that was never meant to be mine to fill. I became my mother’s comfort, her listener, her steady presence. I carried that willingly. I don’t regret loving her. But I now realize I was standing in a place that should have belonged to my father.

And lately, that realization has brought up a lot of grief and anger.

I feel sad for what my mother endured. I feel angry that I had to grow up the way I did. I feel frustrated that so much pain could have been avoided. Sometimes it feels like entire lifetimes of hurt that no one addressed.

But I need you to hear this clearly: none of that is because of you. And you are not competing with anyone.

You once expressed something that has stayed with me — that you don’t want to fight for what is already yours. That you don’t want to feel like you’re snatching a son away from his mother just because you exist. That it’s too heavy to hold.

You are right. You shouldn’t have to hold that.

You are my wife. You are my partner. You are the person I chose to build a life with. My role has shifted — not because I love my mother less, but because marriage changes the structure of loyalty. You are my priority. Not out of obligation, but out of commitment and love.

I already try to show up for you the way I believe a husband should — listening first, protecting your heart, being playful, choosing time alone with you, not raising my voice over small things. I will keep doing that. Not to prove anything. But because that is the kind of marriage I want us to have.

As for my mother — I will love her. I will support her. But I will not make you carry the emotional weight of what happened before you. If we ever pour into her, it will be from strength and unity, not from pressure or guilt. You will never be asked to compete. You will never be asked to shrink.

The anger I feel toward my father is something I am working through. It’s not something I want leaking into our home. I don’t want to repeat what hurt me. I want to build something safe, something warm, something playful and alive with you.

If there are moments you feel hurt, distant, or uneasy, I want you to come to me first. Not because you’re complaining — but because you are my partner. I don’t want you to carry things alone the way I did.

I love my mother.

But I chose you.

And I choose you again, every day.

We are not in competition with anyone. We are building something new.

I love you.
